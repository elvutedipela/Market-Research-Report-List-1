<p><p>The cancer genome sequencing market is primarily divided into two types: second-generation and third-generation sequencing technologies. Second-generation sequencing, also known as next-generation sequencing (NGS), enables high-throughput analysis, providing rapid and cost-effective genomic insights. In contrast, third-generation sequencing offers longer read lengths and improved accuracy, allowing for a deeper understanding of complex genomic structures and real-time analysis. Both technologies play crucial roles in advancing personalized medicine and enhancing cancer research by enabling detailed exploration of genetic alterations in tumors.</p></p>
