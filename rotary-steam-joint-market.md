<p><p>The Rotary Steam Joint market is categorized into two main types: Self-Supported and Bracket/Rod-Supported. Self-Supported joints rely on their own structure for stability and are typically used in applications where space constraints or system design allow for minimal external support. Conversely, Bracket/Rod-Supported joints utilize external brackets or rods for additional support, making them suitable for heavier applications or environments where load-bearing is critical. Both types serve essential roles in pneumatic and hydraulic systems, ensuring smooth operation and flexibility.</p></p>
