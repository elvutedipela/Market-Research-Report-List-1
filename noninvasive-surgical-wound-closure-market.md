<p><p>The noninvasive surgical wound closure market includes two primary types: adhesives and hemostats. Adhesives are specialized glues that bond the skin edges together, effectively sealing wounds without the need for stitches. They promote healing and reduce the risk of infection. Hemostats, on the other hand, are agents used to control bleeding during surgery, facilitating rapid clot formation and minimizing blood loss. Both types emphasize efficiency, patient comfort, and improved recovery outcomes in surgical procedures.</p></p>
