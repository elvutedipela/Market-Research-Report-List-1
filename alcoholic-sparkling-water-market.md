<p><ul><li>Sucrose Fermentation</li><li>Modulated Alcohol</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The alcoholic sparkling water market features two primary types: sucrose fermentation and modulated alcohol. Sucrose fermentation involves the fermentation of sugars, typically from fruits, to create alcohol naturally. This process often results in a beverage with a clean, crisp taste. In contrast, the modulated alcohol market manipulates the alcohol content through various technological methods, allowing for a range of flavor profiles and lower calories without traditional fermentation. Both types cater to consumers seeking refreshing, lower-calorie alcoholic options.</p></p>
